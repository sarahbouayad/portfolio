Who Am I?

Hi I'm Sarah Bouayad

From a non-traditional background to a flourishing career in tech, my journey is a testament to the power of transformation and advocacy. I started in community advocacy, but once I discovered tech, there was no turning back. It’s where I’m meant to be—amplifying BIPOC voices and making tech more accessible. As a software engineer, I have worked on diverse projects with start-ups, and intertwined my passion for nonprofits into actionable work with organizations like UPenn and Heart for Girls. I have developed a deep appreciation for enacting change through tech.

Serving as a Brand Ambassador for Tribaja, Diversitech, and RenderATL, I champion diversity and inclusion within the tech industry. My roles allow me to amplify BIPOC voices and ensure tech spaces are not only innovative but also inclusive.

On the side, I run The Medina Creative, a web development agency that keeps me on the cutting edge of tech. It’s not just about building websites; it’s about creating platforms that empower and inspire.

I am deeply invested in continuous learning and actively contributing to a community that uplifts every member. With each day in tech, I embrace the challenge to learn more and to leverage my position to facilitate growth—not just for myself but for all who are coming up in this exciting industry.
